US Unemployment Rate Analysis

Posted on January 15, 2017 in posts

The United States Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) surveys and calculates the monthly unemployment rate. The unemployment rate is the percentage of individuals in the labor force without a job.

Here is the line plot of unemployment rates from 1948-01-01 and ends at 2016-12-01.

import pandas as pd
%matplotlib inline

unrate = pd.read_csv("unrate.csv")
unrate['DATE'] = pd.to_datetime(unrate['DATE'])
print(unrate.head(12))

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
unrate['MONTH'] = unrate['DATE'].dt.month

fig = plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
colors = ['red', 'blue', 'green', 'orange', 'black']
for i in range(5):
    start_index = i*12
    end_index = (i+1)*12
    subset = unrate[start_index:end_index]
    label = str(1948 + i)
    plt.plot(subset['MONTH'], subset['VALUE'], c=colors[i], label=1948+i)

plt.xlabel('Month Intege')
plt.ylabel('Unemployment Rate, Percent')
plt.title('Monthly Uemploy ent Trends, 1948-1952')
plt.show()
